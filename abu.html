Abu Simbel

Is a famous archaeological site in southern Egypt, near the border with Sudan. It features two massive rock temples built by Pharaoh Ramses II in the 13th century BCE. The larger temple honors Ramses himself, while the smaller one is dedicated to his wife, Queen Nefertari. The temples are known for their colossal statues and impressive carvings. In the 1960s, the entire complex was relocated to higher ground to save it from flooding caused by the construction of the Aswan High Dam.
